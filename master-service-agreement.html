Master Service Agreement

York Computer Repair, Inc. — Internal document

Last Updated: March 2022

Hello and thank you for entrusting York Computer Repair, Inc. ("York Computer," "we," "us," or "our") to provide you with professional information technology services. This Master Services Agreement (this "Agreement") governs our business relationship with you, so please read this document carefully and keep a copy for your records.

Scope

Context

Throughout this Agreement, references to "Client," "you," or "your" mean the entity who has accepted a quote, proposal, service order, or similar document (electronic or otherwise) from York Computer (collectively referred to as a "Quote").

Scope of Services

This is a "master" agreement — specific services are not listed here but will be described in a Quote. The scope is limited to those services expressly listed in a Quote. Services are also further defined in an additional "Services Statement." Please read both the Quote and the Services Statement before accepting the Quote.

Version

Each Quote will be governed under the version of this Agreement in place as of the "last updated" date at the bottom of this document. Keep a copy and note the date when you accept a Quote.

Conflicts

The provisions of a Quote govern over conflicting terms in this Agreement and the Services Statement. Conflicting language between the Services Statement and this Agreement will be interpreted in favor of the Services Statement.

Third Party Providers / Services

Some services are provided directly by our personnel; others are provided by "Third Party Providers." We will select appropriate Third Party Providers for your environment and reserve the right to change them as long as the change does not materially diminish the Services. Third Party Services are provided on an "as is" basis. We are not responsible for any defect, omission, or failure of any Third Party Service. Price increases from Third Party Providers may be passed through to you with as much advance notice as reasonably possible.

Implementation

Advice; Instructions

We may provide specific advice and directions related to the Services. You are strongly advised to promptly follow our advice. We are not responsible for problems caused by your failure to follow our Advice. If your failure makes part or all of the Services economically or technically unreasonable, we may terminate the applicable Services For Cause or adjust the scope of the Quote.

Co-Management

In co-managed situations, we will endeavor to implement the Services efficiently; however, we will not be responsible for the acts or omissions of Co-Managed Providers. If a Co-Managed Provider's determination differs from our position, we will yield to the Co-Managed Provider and bring it to your attention.

Modifications

We strongly recommend that you refrain from modifying or moving the Environment, or installing software, unless we expressly authorize such activity. We will not be responsible for unauthorized changes to the Environment or issues arising from them.

Authorized Contact(s)

We will rely on directions or consent from your designated Authorized Contact(s). Changes to Authorized Contacts provided in writing take effect within 2 business days; changes provided in person or by phone take effect the same day. Do not use a ticketing system or recorded message to change an Authorized Contact.

Access

You hereby grant us and our designated Third Party Providers the right to monitor, diagnose, manipulate, and access the Environment solely as necessary to provide the Services. It is your responsibility to secure, at your own cost, any necessary rights of entry, licenses, or permits prior to commencement of Services.

Response

Our response to issues will be handled per the Quote or Services Statement. We will not be responsible for delays during: the Transition Exception (first 45 days), Scheduled Downtime, Client-Side Downtime, Vendor-Side Downtime, or force majeure events.

Scheduled Downtime

Scheduled maintenance will not occur between 9:00 AM and 5:00 PM Eastern Time, Monday through Friday without your authorization unless exigent circumstances exist. We will provide at least 24 hours of notice before Scheduled Downtime.

Transition Exception

For the first forty-five (45) days following the commencement of any Service, and during any off-boarding period, response time commitments will not apply.

Fees; Payment

Fees

You agree to pay the fees, costs, and expenses charged for the Services as described in each Quote and Services Statement. You are responsible for applicable sales tax, freight, insurance, and import/export duties.

Nonpayment

Fees unpaid for more than 15 days are subject to interest at the lower of 1% per month or the maximum allowable rate. We reserve the right to suspend Services for nonpayment without prior notice. Notice of fee disputes must be received within 60 days after the Service is rendered or the invoice is paid, whichever is later. A reconnect fee of no more than 10% of your monthly recurring fees may apply after suspension.

Increases

Fee increase limits described in the Quote or Services Statement do not apply to Pass Through Increases from Third Party Providers.

Expenses During State of Emergency

Any costs or expenses incurred while providing the Services during a national, state, or local emergency (including fuel or manpower shortages) will be invoiced and payable by you.

Per Seat Licensing Fees

Per seat licenses (such as Microsoft NCE) cannot be canceled once purchased. Regardless of the reason for termination of Services, you are required to pay for all applicable per seat licenses in full for their entire term. You may continue using those licenses until they expire even if you move to a different provider.

Limited Warranties; Limitations of Liability

Hardware / Software Purchases

All equipment purchased through York Computer are generally nonrefundable once obtained from the third party provider. Third party return policies apply. All Third Party Products are provided "as is" without warranty as between York Computer and you.

Liability Limitations

In no event will either party be liable for indirect, special, exemplary, consequential, or punitive damages (including lost revenue or profits) arising out of or in connection with the Services. A Responsible Party's aggregate liability for all claims shall be limited to the lesser of actual and direct damages not exceeding fees paid during the three (3) month period prior to the claim, or $10,000, whichever is greater. These limitations do not apply to willful or intentional misconduct or gross negligence.

Indemnification

Each party agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold the other party harmless from and against any losses, damages, costs, expenses, or liabilities (including reasonable attorneys' fees) that arise from or are related to that party's breach of this Agreement. No claim for which indemnity is sought will be settled without the Indemnifying Party's prior written consent.

Term; Termination

This Agreement

This Agreement is effective as of the date we provide a Service to you or the date you accept a Quote, whichever is earlier. It terminates automatically if terminated For Cause, or six (6) months after the last date on which we provide any Service to you. This Agreement shall not be terminated without cause if Services are in progress under a Quote.

Termination Without Cause

No party will terminate a Quote without cause prior to its natural expiration date. If you terminate without cause, you agree to be responsible for the Termination Fee described below.

Termination For Cause

Either party may terminate For Cause upon a material breach provided: (i) written notice of the specific breach details is given, and (ii) the Defaulting Party has not cured the default within 20 days (10 days for nonpayment) following written notice.

Remedies for Early Termination

If York Computer terminates For Cause, or if you terminate without cause, York Computer is entitled to all amounts that would have been paid had the Agreement remained in full effect through its term ("Termination Fee").

Equipment / Software Removal

Upon termination, you will provide us with access during normal business hours to remove all York Computer Equipment. If access is refused or equipment is missing or damaged, you agree to pay the full replacement value. You agree not to remove or disable any Software Agents we install unless explicitly directed to do so.

Transition; Deletion of Data

Transition assistance is available only after all fees are paid in full and subject to our then-current hourly rates. Software configurations we custom create are our proprietary information and will not be disclosed. We have no obligation to store Client data following termination unless required by applicable law.

Confidentiality

Defined

Confidential Information means all non-public information provided by one party to the other, including customer data, customer lists, internal documents, proprietary reports and methodologies. Excludes information that is public, independently developed, or lawfully received from a third party not under confidentiality obligations.

Use

The Recipient will keep Confidential Information fully confidential and will not use or disclose it to any third party except as expressly authorized, needed to fulfill obligations under this Agreement, or as required by law.

Compelled Disclosure

If legally compelled to disclose Confidential Information, the Recipient will immediately notify the Discloser in writing so that a protective order may be sought. Only the minimum required portion will be disclosed.

Ownership

Each party retains ownership of its Intellectual Property. Nothing in this Agreement grants ownership of one party's Intellectual Property to the other. Any software, codes, or algorithms we create while providing the Services are our property. Third party software is licensed, not sold, and is subject to the applicable EULA.

Arbitration

Except for undisputed collections actions, any dispute arising from or related to this Agreement shall be settled by arbitration before one mutually agreed-upon arbitrator, administered by the American Arbitration Association (AAA). The arbitration will take place at our office. The prevailing party shall be entitled to an award of reasonable attorneys' fees and costs. Initially, arbitration costs are split evenly between the parties.

Miscellaneous

BYOD

You warrant that we are authorized to access all devices connected to the Environment, regardless of ownership. Devices not covered under a managed service plan ("Unknown Devices") are strongly advised not to be connected to the Environment without our prior knowledge.

Compliance

Unless expressly stated in a Quote, the Services are not intended to bring Client into full regulatory compliance. If you are subject to HIPAA or other privacy laws, you agree to identify protected data before providing us access to it.

Virtual Security

No security solution is 100% effective. We do not warrant that all malware will be detected, avoided, or removed, or that data encrypted by malware will be recoverable. Unless caused by our intentionally malicious behavior or gross negligence, we are held harmless from costs arising from malware-related incidents. You are strongly advised to obtain cyber insurance.

Physical Security

You agree to maintain reasonable physical security for all managed hardware, including physical barriers, alarm systems, fire detection/retardant systems, and periodic reviews of personnel access rights.

Non-Solicitation

During the term and for one (1) year after termination, neither party will solicit or recruit the other party's employees with whom they worked. Violation of this provision results in liquidated damages of $100,000 or the amount paid to that employee in the prior year, whichever is greater.

Collections

If we are required to pursue Collections to recover undisputed fees, we are entitled to recover all costs, fees, and reasonable attorneys' fees incurred in the Collections process.

Assignment

Neither this Agreement nor any Quote may be assigned or transferred without the prior written consent of the other party. We may assign our rights in connection with a merger, consolidation, or sale of substantially all of our assets provided the assignee expressly assumes our obligations.

Amendment

This Agreement and any Quote may be amended only by a written document initiated by us that specifically refers to this Agreement or the Quote, and affirmatively accepted in writing by you.

Time Limitations

Unless otherwise prohibited by law, any action arising out of or related to any Service (except nonpayment) must be commenced within six (6) months after the cause of action accrues or it is forever barred.

Force Majeure

Neither party will be liable for delays or failures to perform due to circumstances beyond their reasonable control, including acts of God, natural disasters, acts of terrorism, power failures, cyberwarfare, or malware incidents that circumvent then-current anti-malware software.

No Fiduciary

The scope of our relationship is limited to the specific Services provided. No fiduciary relationship exists or will exist between us. Any fiduciary relationship imposed by operation of law for out-of-scope services is hereby waived.

Governing Law; Venue

This Agreement will be governed by the laws of the state of Pennsylvania. You irrevocably consent to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of York County, Pennsylvania for all non-arbitrable claims.

Notices; Writing Requirement

Notices may be sent by U.S. mail (deemed delivered 3 business days after mailing), overnight courier (1 business day), or email (1 business day). Email notices are sufficient only if sent to the last known designated email address of the recipient.

Independent Contractor

York Computer is an independent contractor, and is not your employer, employee, partner, or affiliate.

Data & Service Access

Some Services may be provided by persons outside of the United States and/or your data may occasionally be accessed or stored on secure servers located outside of the United States. Notify us if your company requires modifications to these standard provisions; additional costs will apply.

Merger

This Agreement, the Quote, and the Services Statement set forth the entire understanding of the parties and supersede all prior agreements related to the Services. Our website and marketing materials are for illustrative purposes only and do not create additional duties, service levels, or guarantees.

Counterparts

The parties may sign, accept, and/or deliver any Quote, this Agreement, or any amendment electronically. Electronic and digital signatures are acceptable and binding.
